Earthquakes and Faults.


- energy makes the ground move, friction hold rocks together
- ground will move and earthquake will occur once friction is overcome

Earthquake
- sudden slip on a fault, released in the form of energy
Creep
- faults/blocks move slowly to each other
Fault - fracture or zone of fractures, may range in length, produce repeated displacements
- Normal - block above moved downward, hanging wall down to the footwall
- Reverse - thrust fault, upper block moves up and over, hanging wall thrust over the footwall, common in areas of compression
- Strike-Slip - blocks slide past each other, San Andreas
- Left lateral - left block moves toward
- Right lateral - right block moves toward
Stress - plates are pushed or pulled together
- Compression - rocks push or squeeze against one another then arise, targets the center, horizontal–crust thickens or shortens, vertical–crust thins out or break off, mountains
- Tension - rocks pull apart, plates can move farther away or a plate can move in different direction, reason why Pangaea were broken off
- Shear - templates rub each other, pushes some of the crust in different directions, can cause earthquakes
- Confining - crust compacts which makes it smaller, stress is applied to all sides of crust, cause sinkholes
