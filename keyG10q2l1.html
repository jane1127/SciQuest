Electromagnetic Theory and Electromagnetic Spectrum.


Scientists contributed:
- Andre-Marie Ampere (AMA) - Earth’s magnetism, Ampere’s Law, solenoid, electrical telegraph, static galvanometer, Ampere unit
- Michael Faraday (MF) - Father of Electricity, principles of electromagnetic induction, invent electric motor, “Electric current produces magnetic field.”
- Heinrich Rudolf Hertz (HRH) - “EM waves exist.”, radio waves (late 1880) — induction coil and receiving antenna, invented radar, photoelectric effect, Hertz frequency
- James Clerk Maxwell (JCM) - Father of Electromagnetic Theory, theory of electromagnetism, color photograph, Maxwell equations
- Hans Christian Oersted (HCO) - “Electric currents create magnetic fields.”, Oersted law, “Moving electrons can create a magnetic field.”, Oersted unit
- Ampere’s Law - magnetic force between two electrical current
- Ampere unit - measures electric current
- Electromagnetic Theory - light was electromagnetic radiation
- Oersted’s Law - steady electric current passing through a wire creates magnetic field
- Oersted unit - magnetic-field strength

Principles of EM wave theory
Mechanical waves - requires a medium to propagate
Light - changing electric and magnetic field that propagate outward
- Light does not require a medium, propagates at 300,000,000m/s (3 x 10^8)
Electromagnetic waves - transverse waves
- Oscillating charge accelerates, moving charge’s electric field changes

Transverse Waves - particles move perpendicular
Electromagnetic Waves - can travel through a medium and vacuum, have the same speed = speed of light, consists of electric and magnetic fields which vibrate – making waves
wavelength ≠ frequency, energy

Parts
Crest - highest part
Trough - lowest part
Wavelength - distance between two waves
Wave height - vertical distance between the trough and crest
Amplitude - maximum height from its rest position

Equation
v = λf (v = voltage, λ = wavelength, f = frequency)

ELECTROMAGNETIC SPECTRUM
complete spectrum (continuum) of all forms of light
wavelength ≠ frequency, energy
Radio Waves - low energy, longest wavelengths, low frequency, FM and AM, transmit sound and picture
Microwaves - satellite communications, radars, TV transmission, microwave oven, can penetrate the Earth’s atmosphere, transmitted by an antenna
Infrared Waves - invisible, detected as heat, night goggles, heat lamps, Frederick William Herschel
Visible Light - human eyes can detect, ROYGBIV, Phototherapy - light in medical treatment
Ultraviolet Waves - higher energy, tanning beds and sterilizing equipment, Vit. D, sterilization of water, identifying original banknotes
X-Rays - Wilhelm Conrad Roentgen, high energy, used in medicine, industry, and astronomy, cause energy
Gamma Rays - highest energy, kill living cells, radiotherapy - treat cancer, sterilization of drinking water
